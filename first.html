Real or Fake : Detecting Deepfake Videos Using Temporal Models for Video Authentication

OVERVIEW


As deepfake videos become increasingly prevalent, reliable methods for distinguishing authentic content from manipulated media are essential. This research explores the use of Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks for deepfake detection by analyzing temporal patterns in video sequences. LSTMs, a type of recurrent neural network (RNN), are particularly effective for this task as they excel at capturing dependencies between frames, making them ideal for detecting discrepancies in facial expressions and movements that are common in manipulated videos. Our approach includes preprocessing video frames to extract key facial landmarks, allowing the LSTM model to better capture the temporal dynamics of facial features. The model is trained on a diverse dataset of both real and deepfake videos, enabling it to learn subtle differences in behavior that distinguish authentic content from manipulated material. To further improve the modelâ€™s accuracy and generalization, we integrate multiple LSTM models in an ensemble framework. Experimental results show that this approach can effectively classify videos as real or fake, outperforming existing detection methods in terms of precision and overall performance. This work advances video authentication technologies and provides a practical solution for ensuring the integrity of digital media. By accurately detecting manipulated content, our method plays a critical role in combating the spread of misinformation and safeguarding the trustworthiness of online visual media.
